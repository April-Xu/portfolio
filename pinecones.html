Mistake Analysis Related Study (MARS)
Our client, Squirrel AI, offers an online learning platform powered by AI in China. One of the key feature is to offer online Mistake Analysis Related Study(MARS) where students correct and analyze their mistakes from practices and study related knowledge components (KC’s). However, MARS currently is treated as a formality of assignments and students only use it to re-practice the problems instead of extracting insights from their mistakes.
Problem Space
How Might We motivate and support students to learn from their most important mistakes during their reflection and review process?
Solution
SOLUTION
A personalized collection of important mistaken problems with intelligent support of reflection and review.
The solution we proposed is the Starred Problem Set feature to be hosted within the MARS system. Students can filter the mistaken problems that are important to them and annotate on them with reflection scaffolding for future review.

UNDERSTANDING USERS
Reduce the frictions and cognitive load for students in doing MARS and help them organize and analyze their mistakes
Based on the primary research we've done with the products, we've narrowed down the problem scope to MARS and identified potential challenges with professionals and competitive products. After that, we conducted semi-structued interviews and contextual inquiries with our primary users - 32 students and 7 teachers. We built identity models and experience maps to explore their challenges and needs, which point to the potential areas of improvement for the online MARS system.
Connect challenges with design strategies
Lack of internal & external motivation
Using MARS as a formality of homework can easily lead to the lack of students’ internal and external motivation.
Can’t see value of MARS with limited metacognitive abilities
There are critical needs of metacognitive abilities for selecting, reviewing, and analyzing their mistaken problems to help students see the value of doing MARS.
Limited time and poor usability in online-offline integration
Time and usability issues of current online-offline integration can be a problem for students to do & review their mistake analysis notebook
Students can easily filter and manage the important mistake problems with the support from instructors and the system
Support metacognitive skill development to lower the thresholds of MARS
Personalize and localize the problem practice and offer online-offline study integration
Ideation
The Starred Question Set can better serve users’ needs and is feasible to implement within the MARS
Based on our HMW statement, we generated over 40 initial ideas through Brainstorming. We then evaluated those ideas in a difficulty & importance matrix, speed-dated them with users and clients to get feedback, and finalized our idea considering design, business, and implementation constraints.
Design Decisions
During the user testing sessions, we interviewed 40+ students to prioritize and iterate on features that: 1. Mimic the offline annotation scenario and also take advantage of intelligent tools; 2. Assist students to review the most important problems and seek help from the professors.
ITERATE ON LEARNING EXPERIENCE
Iterate on the features to enable students to categorize, organize and annotate problems more efficiently
1
Enable students to make annotations in a way that mimics the offline situation and to take advantage of intelligent tools to accelerate the reflection process.
We did user testings to understand students’ habits of making the annotations, what tools they will choose and what will they prioritize. Based on our findings, we designed the toolbox that will enable them to make annotations efficiently.
2
Incorporate student-teacher interaction to the maximum extent without adding too much workload to teachers
Based on our interviews with students and teachers, we found that it’s essential to enable teachers to easily assist students to deepen their understanding of certain concepts and knowledge components and the starred problem set can also serve as a tool for the communication between professors and students. During the iteration, we add several features to satisfy this need.
ITERATE ON USER EXPERIENCE
Iterate to reduce frictions in user flow
1
Iterate on the homepage layout to prioritize core information and make the information architecture explicit
2
Add on-boarding prompts to improve feature discovery and usage
Based on our user testing, we found that users may have difficulties in discovering the features we have during the annotation process We add onboarding prompts to inform users about the features such as auto-fill and leave message for the professors.
We also added hints to the picture-editing feature, to make the functionalities more explicit
