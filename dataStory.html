Project outline



This is a project about loneliness epidemic, a mental health issue that's been worse and worse after COVID19 quarantine.

The major elements of my story will be a hook, an introduction of current situation, an explanation about why loneliness is serious, a section of whose feeling of loneliness are mostly affected by COVID19 and how, a section of how to deal with it, and an appendix for acknowledging data sources.

I expect the readers to think about their current lives at first, be aware of the severeness of the situation, and be able to cope with similar situations after reading my story.

I plan to use Figma, Flourish, and Shorthand to design and develop my project. Figma will only be used for possible artifacts I might creat(e.g. some photo editing or some specific visualization) and Shorthand is used for the website. Flourish will be used for conventional or complicated data visualizations. I will use Figma to wireframe my ideas and redesign the projects based on future user research. I will conduct at least 3 user research, gather their feedback, and make revisions until it's polished and ready for presentation.

Audience



My primary audience is pretty general. Anyone who has access to this website and is affected by the worldwide pandemic would be able to understand and potentially be interested in the content. If specifically, this story would target more on college students who are more concerned about this issue.I did several research to some representative audience and made revisions based on their opinions to make my project work for the general target audience.

Work done so far



Design decisions:
1. I chose the color palatte of black and blue adapting to the topic of being alone under quarantine.
2. I tried to use the minimum amount of text to let my visualizations speak by themseleves.
3. I used infogram to visualize the health risk of being alone in order to make it intuitive and innovative.
4. I browsed through free stock images to find appropriate images that are consistent with the theme but at the same time not distractive.
5. I restructured and cleaned the data I found and created 2 simple visualizations in order to focus on the key information.

Things I've learnt:
1. User research is definitely helpful for finding out blindspots of projects even if there's only a small number of people tested.
2. Present in 60 seconds really enhanced my skill of synthesizing and extracting the core information of my presentation. It's been a very powerful practice that I can use in the future to reduce the unnecessary talkings.

Based on the interviews, I got the following insights:
1. All of the participants are not so aware of the issue, which is representative for a general audience of this website who is experiencing quaratine but not an expert of relevant problems caused.
2. They all identified the key information in the website in the second question but they mentioned different things in question "what does this website tell you", which is surprising to me. I found it interesting that audience will tend to take what they don't know or what they find surprising as what they are told.
3. The visualizations overall is understandable but the details of the typography, labels, and the way the important information is highlighted should be revised because most of the audience found them confusing.
4. The intended audience is fairly clear to all audience.
5. The revisions participants want to make focus more on the later part of the story, which indicates more confusion about the data and call-to-action section.
6. Participants found out some ambiguity about the abstract concepts mentioned in the story, which can lead to confusions about the data.


Moving on, I would do these revisions to my website:
1. Refine the labels and typography of most visualizations to make the information clearer and more concrete.
2. Use Shorthand to make the whole website consistent in terms of style and design including color, layout, fonts, and typography.
3. Reconsider the raw data of "worsened relationship" and "impact of loneliness" and try out different types of visualizations.
4. Conduct more user research about iterated prototypes and redesign.


As final design iterations, I finally implemented the following changes to my data story:
1. Be consistent of overall design including colors, typography, and hierarchy,to create an engaging reading experience.
2. Restructure the data I have and only keep those valuable for my storytelling. Recreate the visualizations in Flourish to make it consistent with the design and conver the information more intuitively.
3. Refine the wording so that it's less confusing and provide more detailed explanation of ambiguous concepts.
4. Use appropriate inetractions in Shorthand to create a immersive experience and created some artifacts needed.
